Overlays

When locating the route of a long-gone streetcar line, often the route travels on private right of way which since has been obscured by subsequent construction. Also, street alignments may have been changed or eliminated due to highway construction, urban renewal or other activities. In such cases, having access to historical maps will allow you to locate where a route ran.

One source of historical maps, in the US is the USGS. They have made many historical topographic maps available on-line, free of charge, at    USGS Map Store. While historical topgraphic maps may be very useful in locating abandoned private right of way, these maps rarely identify city streets (except for main thoroughfares). However, most cities over the years have had numerous maps published showing far more detail than is available on USGS topographic maps. One source of such maps is the David Rumsey Map Collection. This collection has thouands of maps. More importantly, they are georeferenced which means that any point on the map can be specified as a latitude and longitude coordinate. This in turn allows them to be displayed over Mapper's Google Maps or Open Street Map display.

Since these maps are available on line, it is possible to display them as an overlay in Mapper. In order to do this, you must gather some information and setup the map as an overlay in Mapper.

The first thing to do is to find a suitable map. The David Rumsey Map Collection link has a Map Finder. The Map finder displays a red dot for every georeferenced map in the collection. So, zoom in and click on a dot. The details of the map will be displayed:
You can then click on the map to open it as an overlay on a Google maps Now, in the upper right hand of this screen, click on "Embed OGC WMTS tiles". The next screen will be displayed with one or more rectangular boxes. Click on the one titled "Polynomial". Next will be shown the selected map displayed as an overlay on Google Maps. In the right hand column of the screen is a list of Web viewers. The one titled "Google Maps Api (KlokanTech) should be highlighted in gray. " Click on "Source Code".

This is the HTML code that we need to extract some information to be entered into the Update Overlay Information dialog. Select "Edit Overlay List" from the Tools menu.
  1. From the HTML, enter the minimum and maximum zoom levels.
  2. Fill in the latitude and longitude of the map bounds from the mapBounds defintion in the HTML: 
    var mapBounds = new google.maps.LatLngBounds(
                      new google.maps.LatLng(39.069253, -84.570544),
                      new google.maps.LatLng(39.174521, -84.472617));
  3. Fill in the url information from the HTML. This is one or more URLs separated by commas between the first pair of square brackets in the return statement. Enter each url on a new line in the Url text edit box. Hint: replace all occurrences of '", "' with line-feed and then delete any remaining double quotes.
